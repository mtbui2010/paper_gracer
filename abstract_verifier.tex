\begin{abstract}
Plan-quality metrics commonly reported for LLM-based embodied
planners hinge on a heuristic precondition test --- e.g.\ ``every
\texttt{Pick} is preceded by some \texttt{Navigate} or \texttt{Find}''
--- that is easy for a fluent LLM to satisfy on the surface but does
not enforce object identity, holding state, or container open/close
order. When we replace this heuristic with a strict symbolic
verifier, baselines that previously appeared to score $1.0$
precondition routinely drop to $0.4$--$0.7$, revealing that their
plans are silently invalid. We propose \textbf{GRACE} (Grounded
Repair-and-Critique Embodied planner), a single-LLM planner whose
contribution is three-fold: (i) a rule-based symbolic precondition
verifier ($\sim$250 lines of Python, zero tokens, $O(|\pi|)$ time)
that gates each sub-plan and emits typed violations driving a single
refinement pass; (ii) hierarchical decomposition with
sub-goal--localised repair; and (iii) a hybrid memory store seeded
from ground-truth plans and grown by successful episodes. On the
38-task AI2-THOR household benchmark across three open-weight Ollama
models, GRACE is the only method that maintains a strict
precondition score above $0.85$ on every model, while simultaneously
achieving the highest goal completeness ($+0.10$--$0.13$ over the
best baseline). We discuss the cost--quality trade-off honestly: the
verifier and memory add a constant overhead that buys reliability the
baselines cannot match by simply spending more tokens. Code and
benchmark scripts are released.
\end{abstract}
