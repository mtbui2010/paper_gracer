\title{GRACE: Lightweight Symbolic Verification and \\
Local Repair for LLM Task Planning in Embodied Agentss}

\author{First A. Author${}^{1}$ and Second B. Author${}^{2*}$ }

\affils{ ${}^{1}$Department of Electrical Engineering, Hankook University, \\
Seoul, 13391, Korea (first@hankook.ac.kr) \\
${}^{2}$Department of Mechanical Engineering, Hankook University, \\
Seoul, 13391, Korea (second@hankook.ac.kr) {\small${}^{*}$ Corresponding author}}


\abstract{
    Large language models (LLMs) are now the default cognitive core of
embodied household agents, but plans produced by current methods
average $2$--$3\times$ more steps than the simulator-verified
reference and rely on retry loops to recover from precondition
violations the planner never catches. We propose \textbf{GRACE}
(Grounded Repair-and-Critique Embodied planner), a single-LLM planner
that integrates three lightweight components: (i) a rule-based
symbolic precondition verifier that runs in $O(|\pi|)$ time and zero
tokens, emitting typed violations that drive a single low-temperature
refinement pass; (ii) hierarchical decomposition with
\emph{sub-goal--localised} repair — when a step fails, GRACE
regenerates only the suffix of the failed sub-goal while keeping
completed work and untouched future sub-goals intact; and (iii) a
hybrid memory store seeded from a curated pool of ground-truth plans
and grown by successful runtime episodes, with embedding retrieval
when available and a Jaccard fallback otherwise. On a 38-task
AI2-THOR household benchmark across four open-weight Ollama models
(\texttt{llama3.2}, \texttt{qwen2.5:7b}, \texttt{mistral-nemo},
\texttt{qwen2.5:14b}) and eight published planning strategies as
baselines, GRACE attains the lowest plan length / reference ratio and
the highest precondition-satisfaction rate, with the largest gains
observed at the small-model end of the size axis. Ablations confirm
that each component contributes a distinct, non-overlapping
improvement. Code and benchmark scripts are released as a
self-contained artifact.
}
